The IGNOU question, specifically

An IGNOU M.Com or MBA is cheap, UGC-recognised, and can run quietly in the background without eating portfolio hours. It will not add a single Australian point and will not change your German eligibility. Do it only if (a) it costs you nothing emotionally, (b) it never displaces a portfolio or English hour, and (c) you accept it is a hedge, not a lever. If you ever feel "I should pause projects to focus on the degree," drop the degree.

Plan B trigger, written down now so future-you cannot fudge it

If by June 2028 you have an English score of 7+, six-plus public projects, an active application pipeline, and still zero interviews or sponsorship interest, then open the German M.Sc. route: apply Nov 2028-Jan 2029 for Winter 2029 intake (uni-assist, ZAB statement ≈ €208, blocked account ≈ €13K/yr). It converts your migration problem into a student-visa problem, which is structurally easier, and graduating in Germany unlocks the lower Blue Card threshold and an 18-month job-seek permit. Until that trigger fires, the answer to "should I do a Master's?" is no.

🇦🇺 Australia: the three engines, ranked for you

1 · Skills in Demand visa (482)

Your most realistic engine. Employer sponsors you; no points test. Core Skills stream needs an occupation on the CSOL, 1+ yr relevant experience, modest English (IELTS 5.0 overall), and pay of at least the Core Skills Income Threshold: AUD 76,515 now, AUD 79,499 for nominations lodged from 1 Jul 2026 (indexed yearly). Up to 4 years; PR via 186 after 2 years with the sponsor. Applicant fee AUD 3,210; most sponsor costs sit with the employer.

No points contestNeeds a job offer

2 · State 190 / Regional 491

The parallel lottery ticket. Points-tested PR (190) or 5-year regional provisional → PR via 191 (491, +15 points). Minimum 65 points, but 2025-26 rounds invited some occupations at 65 and others at 90+. Fee AUD 4,910. A points-test redesign is in progress for 2026-27: re-model your score every year, never assume last year's math.

PR directly (190)Competitive + reform risk

3 · ENS 186

The PR landing after 482. Direct employer-nominated permanent residence, normally after 2 years with your sponsor. Same CSIT salary floor applies. This is how the 482 engine ends in a passport-track outcome: 482 (2 yrs) → 186 (PR) → citizenship after 4 years of residence including 12 months as PR.

🇩🇪 Germany: entry is easy, the job market is the wall

1 · Chancenkarte (entry)

12-month job-seek residence. Qualify via a recognised degree (direct route) or 6 points (age, experience, language). Baseline language: German A1 or English B2. Proof of funds €13,092 (€1,091/month, 2026) in a blocked account; fee €75. Part-time work to 20 h/week + 2-week job trials allowed. A follow-up card of up to 2 more years exists if you hold a qualifying offer.

Move first, search on-ground

2 · Skilled Worker visa §18a/b (your likely conversion)

Once you land an offer matching your recognised qualification, this is the realistic permit: no Blue-Card-level salary bar, pay must simply be standard for the role. Needs your B.Com recognised (anabin H+ university; get the ZAB Statement of Comparability, ≈ €208, in advance). Settlement permit typically after ~3 years with pension contributions and German skills.

Most ops salaries route here

3 · EU Blue Card (if salary clears)

2026 thresholds: €50,700 standard; €45,934 for shortage occupations, recent graduates (degree <3 yrs old, not you) and experienced IT specialists without a degree. Ops/coordinator roles usually pay below this; senior automation/AI roles can clear it. PR after 27 months, or 21 with German B1.

Bonus, not the plan
Two honest 2026 corrections to your old file: (1) Germany's 3-year fast-track citizenship was abolished in 2025; plan on 5 years to naturalisation, dual citizenship still permitted. (2) Don't budget your German salary expectations against the Blue Card; budget against the Skilled Worker visa, which most €45-60K operations offers will use. Germany only enters your active plan if, by end of Year 2, you genuinely hold German B1 and still want it. Otherwise it stays a parked runway, and that is a fine outcome.

The four strategies

1 · LinkedIn engine

  • Headline: role + proof, e.g. "AI Operations Specialist · I build n8n + LLM systems that save ops teams 10+ hrs/week".
  • 2 posts/week from Year 2: one build log, one lesson. 200+ posts by Year 4 outranks any resume.
  • Featured section: 3 best projects + the playbook. Comment thoughtfully on AU/DE recruiters' posts for 2 weeks before connecting.
  • Creator mode on; tools tagged (n8n, LangChain, Claude) so search finds you.

2 · Resume, per market

  • Australia: 2-3 pages is normal; achievement bullets with numbers; no photo, no age, no marital status; spell it "organisation".
  • Germany: CV (Lebenslauf) 1-2 pages, clean reverse-chronological; photo now optional, skip it; short tailored cover letter still matters.
  • Every bullet = verb + system + measured outcome. "Built WATI broadcast automation serving 40K contacts, cutting send-prep time 70%."
  • One master resume, then a tailored variant per application. ATS-safe formatting, no tables in the body.

3 · Portfolio presentation

  • GitHub profile README = a landing page: who you help, 6 pinned projects, one architecture diagram, contact.
  • Each repo: Problem → Build → Stack → Outcome-with-number → Next. Screenshots or a 60-second Loom beat paragraphs.
  • One canonical case-study post per flagship project, cross-posted to LinkedIn.
  • Recruiters skim for 90 seconds. Design for the skim; reward the dig.

4 · Networking quotas

  • Y1-2: 8-10/mo Indian expats in AU tech ops (ask about their journey, never for referrals first) · 2-3/mo Indians in DE ops.
  • Y3: 4-5 AU recruiters/mo + 3-4 DE recruiters at English-first companies; warm up via comments first.
  • Y4: 2-3 hiring managers/mo, opened with a relevant project, value first.
  • Boards: Seek, LinkedIn AU, Indeed AU · StepStone, XING, Join.com, EnglishJobs.de, Berlin Startup Jobs, the make-it-in-germany job board.
The referral math that justifies all of this: one warm referral from an AU-based connection converts better than a hundred cold applications. The quotas above produce roughly 150-200 genuine relationships by Year 4; you only need three of them to vouch for you once.

Six golden rules

1 · Morning = hard skill

Retention is highest before work. IELTS writing and German grammar live at 6:30, video lectures live at night.

2 · Never skip the morning

On bad days, do only the 30-minute morning block. Daily 30 beats weekend 180, every time, forever.

3 · One subject per session

Never split a block between two subjects. Context switching is where retention goes to die.

4 · Mock every two weeks

English: full timed test. Data: mini project. German: 10-minute recording. Measured, not felt.

5 · Log every day

One line: what, how long, one thing learned. In a year this log is your discipline evidence and your story.

6 · Commute = ears

Listening practice or language audio, every commute. It is 150+ free hours a year; claim them.
